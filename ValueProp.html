Value Proposition: Problem Statement and Benefits

Quantifying Adaptive Security for Modern Platforms

Translating bare-metal federated learning into measurable risk reduction, revenue protection, and platform trust.

Problem statement

Modern platforms face a rapidly expanding class of below‑OS threats that traditional OS‑centric security tools cannot see or quantify. Zero‑day exploits, firmware tampering, sideband controller abuse, hardware‑level manipulation, and persistent implants now drive the highest‑impact incidents—operating beneath the operating system, evading EDR, and eroding platform trust across entire fleets.

Side‑channel leakage and fault‑injection attacks further widen this blind spot. These techniques create subtle anomalies in timing, power, cache behavior, and early‑boot execution—signals that OS‑level tools are fundamentally unable to observe. Bare‑metal federated learning restores this missing visibility by learning normal hardware and firmware patterns across the fleet and detecting deviations that indicate leakage, glitching, tampering, or early‑boot compromise.

Scope of the Problem

Modern platforms face a growing class of below‑OS threats—firmware tampering, sideband abuse, hardware implants, side‑channel leakage, fault‑injection attacks, and zero‑day exploitation—that traditional OS‑centric security tools cannot see or quantify. These attacks operate beneath the operating system, evade EDR, manipulate early‑boot and hardware behavior, and undermine platform trust across entire fleets.

Size of the Problem

Below‑OS threats—firmware tampering, sideband controller abuse, hardware implants, side‑channel leakage, fault‑injection attacks, and zero‑day exploitation—now drive the highest‑severity incidents across cloud, enterprise, and device ecosystems. These attacks compromise the layers that determine platform integrity, enabling persistence beneath the OS, evasion of EDR, manipulation of early‑boot logic, and silent degradation of hardware behavior across entire fleets.

The business impact is substantial: below‑OS compromises lead to fleet‑wide outages, device recalls, SLA penalties, and supply‑chain contamination, creating disproportionate revenue exposure. Firmware and silicon vulnerabilities routinely delay platform launches by months, extending time‑to‑market and eroding competitive advantage. Detection and response at these layers are 5–10× slower than OS‑level incidents, with exposure windows often measured in weeks or months—leaving organizations blind to the most consequential classes of compromise.

How Our Solution Closes the Gap

Bare‑metal federated learning provides the missing visibility layer. By learning directly from hardware, firmware, and sideband telemetry—across fleets and without exporting sensitive data—it detects the classes of attacks that OS‑level tools cannot observe. This reduces MTTD by 20–50×, cuts MTTR by 5–10×, and shrinks blast radius by up to 90%, directly improving platform reliability, accelerating time‑to‑market, and protecting revenue.

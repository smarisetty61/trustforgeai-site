Director of Business Development: AI Infrastructure & Hardware Security

As the Director of Business Development, you will spearhead the strategic growth of design and engineering services for 2025-era AI and cloud infrastructure. This executive-level role focuses on selling specialized services—spanning BIOS/BMC/Root of Trust (RoT) firmware and advanced AI security—to Tier 1 Cloud Service Providers (CSPs), OEMs, and ODMs. You will bridge the gap between hardware-level technical depth and the evolving security requirements for massive AI clusters and autonomous systems.

Key Responsibilities

  • AI Infrastructure Expansion: Drive revenue by selling specialized engineering services for AI-optimized hardware, including high-density GPU clusters and Liquid Cooling-ready server designs.
  • Security Solution Selling: Position and sell services focused on Hardware Root of Trust, secure boot, attestation, and the protection of AI models at the edge and in the data center.
  • Standards & Compliance Advocacy: Ensure service offerings align with 2025 industry requirements, including the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF) and the Open Compute Project (OCP) security specifications.
  • Ecosystem Orchestration: Serve as the primary liaison between CSP infrastructure architects and ODM manufacturing teams to ensure global execution of secure platform programs.
  • Strategic Pipeline Management: Lead the full sales cycle from initial technical discovery and RFP responses to the negotiation of multi-million dollar service contracts.
  • AI Governance Integration: Translate 2025 regulatory trends, such as AI-SBOM (Software Bill of Materials) mandates and model-integrity verification, into new business opportunities.

Required Qualifications

  • Industry Experience: 10+ years of leadership in Business Development or Technical Sales within the data center, semiconductor, or server manufacturing sectors.
  • Technical Expertise:
    • Firmware & Management: Deep knowledge of BIOS/UEFI, BMC (OpenBMC/AMI), and DC-SCM modules.
    • Hardware Security: Direct experience with Silicon RoT, Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs), and Platform Firmware Resiliency (NIST SP 800-193).
    • AI Security: Proven understanding of securing AI pipelines, including defense against model poisoning and protecting inference at scale.
  • Standards Mastery: Demonstrated experience with ISO/IEC 42001, DMTF (SPDM/MCTP), and FIPS certification processes.
  • Executive Network: An active roster of decision-makers at Tier 1 CSPs (e.g., AWS, Azure, GCP, Meta) and global ODMs (e.g., Quanta, Foxconn, Wiwynn).
  • Education: B.S. in Electrical Engineering, Computer Science, or Cybersecurity. An MBA or advanced technical degree is highly preferred.

Preferred Skills & Competencies

  • 2025 Market Insight: Familiarity with the 2025 U.S. AI Action Plan and global AI assurance mandates.
  • Technical Negotiation: Success in closing complex, long-cycle contracts involving IP licensing and NRE (Non-Recurring Engineering) services.
  • Communication: Exceptional ability to translate "low-level" hardware security risks into "high-level" business value for C-suite stakeholders.
  • Mobility: Ability to travel up to 40% to engage with ODM partners in Asia and CSP hubs globally.
